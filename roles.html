In this portion of the analysis, we looked into how different roles and their respective job families faired during the pandemic. On the right, is a small graphic showing basic statistics on the dataset. The main question that we aimed to answer was:

How did each job family perform over the course of the dataset?
What roles performed the best and worst?
How did senior job roles fare as compared to entry-level positons?
What are potential reason for the trends?

Almost all job families had a negative overall job posting proportion over the period the data was collected. Manufacturing/Operations Staff had an average performance of almost 0 while Facilities/Construction had a slight increase of 0.1. It makes sense that most job postings performed worse relative to what they were right before COVID because of the lockdown and stay at home orders.

How do we define High and Low?
From the table above, there are over 1,000 different roles within the dataset. Here, we attempted to classify "High" roles as any role containing "Senior", "Executive", "President", and similar upper management type job titles. On the other end of the spectrum, we classified "Low" roles as any role containing "Entry", "Intern", "Junior", and similar "Low" job titles.
What does this mean?
Seperated by job family, each of the bars are split into two portions. The blue pertains to the number of job postings pertaining to a high job role in that job family. Likewise, the orange pertains to the number of job postings pertaining to the low job role in that job family. It is clear that Finance/Insurance had the most roles high roles overall. It's also clear that there are not as many low role job postings compared to high role job postings. This could be due to the lack of data, those roles are offered less, or having other naming conventions.

Why Each Role Increased

Why Real Estate Manager roles increased: Since people who own property can't necessarily travel to their properties, they would hire someone who is near or onsite that could manage it for them.

Why Bakery Supervisor roles increased: This one isn't obvious why it would increase. This culd be due to COVID making people stay at home and more people explore their baking passion. So, when things began to open again, people started their own baking business and required a bakery supervisor.

Why Busperson roles increased: In 2021 there was a huge bus driver shortage. This shortage was already present, but it was made even worse by COVID. Therefore, there was a huge need for bus drivers and more job postings.

Why Bricklayer roles increased: Residential construction increased 14.9% which could be the cause of the Bricklayer role increase. Otherwise, I'm not exactly sure why.

Why Advertising Manager roles increased During 2021, there was an overall increase of 9.1% in marketing specifically. Over 17% of those were going to Senior Managers.

Why Each Role Decreased

Why Tax Professionals Decreased so much: I believe there are two reasons that Tax Professionals decreased so much compared to before covid.

  1. The tax deadline was extended to May 17th in 2021 due to COVID.
  2. Around 80% of Americans file their taxes using either TurboTax or without any assistance at all.

Why Kitchen Manager jobs decreased: Since many restaurants were closed during covid or only doing delivery/takeout, there wasn't as much of a need for Kitchen Managers because there wasn't as much of a time crunch for getting food out all together/on time.

Why Sales roles decreased: Similarly to above, many stores closed for quite awhile and therefore didn't need sales people. Even when they did reopen, there were limited people allowed in stores, so they didn't require as many sales people.

Why Auto Mechanic roles decreased: Since many places were closed and many people stayed at home, they didn't drive their vehicles and therefore didn't need to get maintenance done.

Why Team Member roles decreased: This one is more difficult to figure out because the roles aren't specific to an industry. It's hard to pinpoint what being a Team Member even means. Even so, we can assume that during COVID anyone who might call themselves a team member (Grocery, clothing, department, etc. store workers) weren't working and therefore there were less postings for them.
