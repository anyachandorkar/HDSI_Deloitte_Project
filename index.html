What data were we given?

We were given seven different datasets each containing at least the following two columns:

  • post_date: A date between March 1st, 2020 and September 20th, 2021 when the job was posted.
  • count_id_indexed: The unique job listings for the dataset for a seven day period divided by the unique number of job listings for the dataset from February 24, 2020 to March 1, 2020.
How do we define job performance?

We define a count-id-index value of 1 to mean that the job industry, family, or role performed the same relative to right before COVID-19 began. In two of our analysis, we standardized the proportions within their groups in order to better understand the differences relative to their overall distribution.

What is our goal?

Out of the seven datasets, one was an overall look into job postings, and the rest were split into four categories which we formed our questions around. These four categories and questions can be viewed below.

Geography:
  • What states were doing better comparitively in each month of our dataset?
  • Which states had the highest difference in jobs from 2020 to 2021?
  • Which industries performed well in which states?
  • Which states performed poorly overall and in particular industries?
Industries:
  • What industry impacts the job economy the most?
  • Which industries are correlated to each other?
  • Which industries do not affect each other?
  • What are the best performing indsutries over time
Roles & Job Families:
  • How did each job family perform over the course of the dataset?
  • What roles performed the best and worst?
  • How did senior job roles fare as compared to entry-level positons?
  • What are potential reason for the trends?
Time Series:
  • What trends are constant over time
  • What irregularities do we see in certain points in time
  • Can we forecast an accurate prediction for the last 3 months of 2021
  • What type of model is best fit on the kind of data we have
